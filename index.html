Think about the common things that you use an elevator. List your most common uses and orther more rare uses. Does the interface make doing those common things easier?

Common Uses:

  • Button Floor Levels
  • Open and Close Buttons

Uncommon Uses:

  • Bells Button
  • Call Button
  • Fire Fighter Button (Emergency Button)

Common Sequence of Actions:

  • Walk into elevator.
  • Turn around and find designated floor option.
  • Push the floor button, wait for doors to close.
  • If doors dont close fast enough, click the door close button.
  • Wait while elevator moves either up or down.
  • When doors open back up, leave elevator.

How does the elevator support the user to figure out how to make it work?

By providing all available floor buttons on one interface panel, to give options to the user to help find where your current destination could be.

How does the elevator provide feedback to the user?

After button the user chooses is pressed, it glows bright red on the out circles of the button, and the above screen shows a confusing arrow and numbers changing.

My Low Fidelity Prototype Sketch

My Design Decisions:

My Design Decisions: I added a voice button allowing users to speak to the control panel for a designated floor option (this is and accessibility feature), I made the buttons more visibly pleasing and linear, of the main control panel from the left starts with floor one and underneath all the main numbers in the middle represents the basement for clarity. The open and close door buttons are closer together near the basement button underneath all the primary floor buttons. The bell and emergency buttons are not spread out allowing the user in a panicked situation to be able to click both at the same time and allow to call for emergency services. Also on the physical buttons i provided the braille so users would not have to find the button after finding the correct location of the braille, the braille is actually on the button so once they find it they can just push!
